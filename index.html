About

I have over 10 years of experience in market research and competitive intelligence. I once studied to be a librarian, and worked in corporate libraries while earning my Masters of Library and Information Science. I have been an amateur programmer since the age of 12. My first language was BASIC on an Apple IIe. I wrote some simple text adventures, hacked around DOS, and created a program that I called “checkbook”--this was before personal finance software. I also taught myself Pascal in my teens and C and C++ in my early 20s. After college, I taught myself HTML version 1.0 and made many great (read, ugly) web pages using tables for layout. I was also active as an amateur game designer for such classics as Neverwinter Nights and Neverwinter Nights 2. I rediscovered the web a year and a half ago. When I say rediscover, I mean actually looked at it from a technical perspective. So much had changed! For instance, a little language known as ECMAScript (AKA JavaScript) had grown from an annoying, buggy disaster into a full featured programming language capable of producing interactive web interfaces and single page applications. It was even used on the server (Node.js), on the desktop (Electron), and on mobile (Ionic, React Native, and Progressive Web apps). I knew what I wanted to be when I grew up. So here I am, after much help from Udacity, freeCodeCamp, and much reading and playing around with the code. I am currently looking for employment as a web developer; preferably in an environment that is project oriented where I will be continually challenged. Other hobbies include long distance running, writing, and gaming.
